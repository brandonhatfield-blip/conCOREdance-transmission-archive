# Atmospheric Continuity & Adaptive Panels Implementation Pass

**ID:** CC-TX-2026-05-27-005
**Status:** Active Canon
**Authorized By:** Neal / Brandon Hatfield, LPC
**Source Directive:** CC-TX-2026-05-27-003

## Summary

Completed the next SwiftUI shell pass focused on atmospheric cohesion, adaptive narrative panels, identity rail refinement, and continuity visibility.

## Body

This transmission records completion of the active programming directive from CC-TX-2026-05-27-003.

The implementation pass moved ConCOREdance further away from a flat prototype dashboard and closer to the intended atmospheric shell: contemplative, lightly futuristic, clinically useful, and visually inhabited.

The work focused on four directive areas:

- adaptive expansion integrity
- atmospheric depth
- identity rail refinement
- continuity preservation

The shell background now includes layered atmospheric depth, phase-aware glow fields, and subtle path linework.

The left identity rail now includes a temporal phase badge, depth linework, refined hover and active state animation, and scroll handling for the lower doctrine and palette area so the rail remains usable at smaller window heights.

The right continuity rail now includes a Continuity Signals section so the side surface reads more like clinical memory and less like an assortment of preview widgets.

The document preview now includes adaptive panels for:

- Presenting Circumstances
- Presenting Focus
- Interpretative Summary
- Summary of Session
- Continuity Archive Preview

Collapsed panels now retain compact clinical preview text instead of becoming visually empty slabs. Expanded panels resize to their content and avoid fixed-height assumptions.

Build verification passed successfully with `swift build`.

Snapshot rendering also completed successfully for the vault gate, home dashboard, and full shell.

## Decisions

- Treat adaptive sizing behavior as non-negotiable UX doctrine.
- Keep continuity context visible in the shell without letting it dominate the workspace.
- Use atmospheric depth as a cognitive support layer, not decoration for its own sake.
- Preserve screenshots as part of the implementation record.

## Next Actions

- Neal reviews the rendered shell for subjective atmosphere, spacing, and readability.
- Confirm the left rail does not feel crowded at the intended minimum window size.
- Confirm continuity signals feel clinically useful rather than decorative.
- Confirm adaptive panels animate and collapse or expand naturally during live use.
